The Quantum Mechanics Behind the Game

This game acts as a macro-level simulation of quantum physics. Traditional tic-tac-toe is classical—bits are strictly 1 or 0. Here, we use qubits.

1. Superposition

When you place a move (e.g., X₁) into two cells at once, that piece exists in a superposition. It is simultaneously in both locations until an observation forces it to choose.

2. Entanglement

If you place a new move into a cell that already contains a quantum piece, those two moves become entangled. Their states are now linked; deciding the outcome of one instantly determines the outcome of the other.

3. Measurement & Collapse

When a loop of entanglements (a cyclic graph) forms, it creates a paradox that reality must resolve. This triggers a wave-function collapse. The player forces a measurement, causing the entire entangled chain to instantly snap into a single, definite classical state.
